# Just The Boss Fights - 
<iframe style="margin:auto; display:block;" width="560" height="315" src="https://video.fastly.steamstatic.com/store_trailers/257120712/movie480_vp9.webm?t=1743458241" title="Steam video player" frameborder="0" allow="accelerometer; clipboard-write; encrypted-media; gyroscope; picture-in-picture; web-share" referrerpolicy="strict-origin-when-cross-origin" allowfullscreen></iframe>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Just The Boss Fights is a side-scroller boss rush game that forces players to quickly adapt to both new abilities and enemies as the player skips throughout their bugged game to only experience the boss fights. Each boss fight comes with not only new attacks and boss mechanics, but an entirely new loadout for the player to quickly learn at the same time. The game was a larger scale project both in time and team size, with a development time of about 7 months from prototyping to release and a team of roughly 30 people all working on it.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Throughout development we focussed on creating the underlying systems we could continue to use before refining that content, and then created a vertical slice to work as our standard from then on. I played several roles throughout development, ranging from establishing early systems and mechanics to implementing content and subsequently debugging and polishing. My focus early on was creating a controller for scenes and ensuring it could easily communicate with the other parts of the game, thus allowing one to easily move between scenes and for another script to access relevant data. For the later half of development I generally worked with our sound designer and composer to implement the audio middleware Wwise mid-development and connect their sound system with the disparate parts of our codebase, ensuring nothing in our game could cause audio bugs or play sounds at unexpected times.
